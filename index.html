Introducing the Mentor Helping Mentor Series

Unlocking Potential

Dear Future Mentors,

When I graduated from university, I realized something that stayed with me—there were countless skills, life lessons, and opportunities I could have explored while in school, but I focused solely on academics and grades. While academic success is important, life after graduation demands more than just good grades. The ability to adapt, innovate, and develop essential skills outside the classroom often sets individuals apart in the real world.

As an undergraduate, I did what I could to survive. I sold local network data, took on cleaning and laundry services, and later co-founded a tutorial center that provided math, computing, and science support to students at all levels. These experiences shaped me, but I often look back and think – what if I had done more? What if someone had guided me to expand my perspective beyond coursework and embrace skills that would propel me forward in life?

Today, as a PhD student in Applied and Computational Mathematics, I am launching the Mentor Helping Mentor Series (MHMS) – a long-term initiative designed to empower undergraduate students across Nigeria to live to their fullest potential. This program is built on the belief that while individual growth is essential, collective growth is transformative.

Learn More About MHMS

Why This Matters

The Mentor Helping Mentor Series aims to create a network of students supporting one another through mentorship, shared experiences, and the pursuit of personal and professional development. We believe that every student can be a mentor in their own right, offering value to their peers while receiving guidance from those ahead of them.

This program is designed to:

  • Equip students with essential skills for life beyond university – leadership, entrepreneurship, communication, and adaptability.
  • Foster a culture of mentorship and community building where knowledge is passed down and multiplied.
  • Help students discover and secure scholarships as undergraduates and prepare for fully funded opportunities abroad after graduation.
  • Encourage students to showcase their skills, learn from others, and teach what they know, creating a cycle of growth and empowerment.

What You’ll Gain

  • Scholarship Guidance: Learn how to apply for and win undergraduate and postgraduate scholarships, opening doors to study abroad.
  • Skill Development: Gain practical, hands-on experience and learn new skills that can generate income or boost your professional profile.
  • Community of Mentors: Engage with peers and senior mentors who will guide, inspire, and walk alongside you on this journey.
  • Opportunities to Lead: Take ownership of initiatives, co-lead workshops, and create meaningful impact.
  • Holistic Growth: Develop personally, professionally, and academically to prepare for life after graduation.
